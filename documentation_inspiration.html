Documentation Inspiration

By Corey Hensley, IDM 221-002

Website One: IGN.com

IGN.com is a fun, informative and well structured site that gives its visitors a vast amount of content.

Pros about IGN

  • The various categories are well thought out and organized.
  • Links for the each category are located in more than one location, making finding content much easier.

Cons about IGN

  • Pop up advertisements can become annoying
  • Sometimes article backgrounds don't load, which causes the site's graphical background image to blend into the an article's content making the information harder to read.

IGN.com is an aggregator and creator of news related content, guides and reviews of all the things I am interested in, film, television video games and various tech devices. It presents all its a content in a sleek, engaging package.

Website Two: Boxofficemojo.com

Boxofficemojo.com is a simple, content oriented site that contains finanical statistics about films and the overall box office.

Pros about Box Office Mojo

  • Box Office Moho is all about the content, and the content categories and sub categories are vast.
  • The site has a simple user interface design.

Cons about Box Office Mojo

  • The site's design is just a little too simple. It could use a little polish.
  • It's surprising that there isn't a lot more news about the industry. Stats are fine but if the site had more industry content, the frequency of visitors to the site would increase.

Boxofficemojo.com has been around for years and it's design has never changed. This site inspires me to think of ways to redesign its user interface and add more content.

Website Three: Headhunterhairstyling.com

Headhunterhairstyling.com appears to have had a couple of redesigns, and the latest hasn't gotten much better.

Pros about Headhunter Hair Styling

  • There isn't much to like about this site, save for the logo. It has potential.

Cons about the Headhunting Hair Styling

  • Most of the imagery is low resolution, and ones that aren't look like they are stock imagery from the internet;
  • The image slider on the home page is far bigger than it needs to be, and the fade to white is cliche.
  • The color palettes used for this site are not at all complimentary to each other and don't really convey the sleekness needed for this type of establishment.
  • The Services page is actually using clip art; this makes for a tacky display.
  • And why is a blog needed for this site?

This site does almost everything wrong when it comes to concepts and rules of design. There is a possibility that customers who visit this will be turned off from this business. It would almost be better if they didn't have a site at all.
